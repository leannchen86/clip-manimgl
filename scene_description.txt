Diagonal cells light up in a warm color (high similarity, correct pairs), while off-diagonal cells cool to dark blue (low similarity, incorrect pairs). Show two loss arrows: one labeled L_image sweeping across rows, another labeled L_text sweeping down columns, then averaging into a single L_CLIP label at the center.
Scene 3: L2 Normalization & Cosine Similarity on a Hypersphere
Start in 2D with a raw embedding vector from the origin whose tip lies outside the unit circle. Animate the vector shrinking or stretching onto the unit circle while keeping its angle fixed. Label the formula briefly: normalized vector equals original vector divided by its norm. Then transition into a 3D sphere or a strong 2.5D illusion of a sphere, with several normalized embeddings shown as glowing points on the surface. Highlight two points and draw their angle from the center, labeling theta. Then show cosine similarity as depending only on that angle. Animate two points moving closer in direction and then farther apart. Keep one line of plain-language text: “After normalization, direction matters more than raw magnitude.”

Scene 5: MLP Decision Boundaries
Start with a 2D scatter plot of two interleaved classes (red and blue points). A single straight line tries to separate them but fails — label it "linear classifier: insufficient." Fade in a small MLP diagram on the side: input layer → hidden layers (with ReLU activation illustrated as a bent line graph) → output layer. As each layer processes the data, animate the feature space warping — the points rearrange via a smooth transformation at each layer, until in the final latent space they become linearly separable. A clean decision boundary line appears in this transformed space, then maps back to the original space as a curved, non-linear boundary weaving between the classes. End by annotating each region with the predicted class label, and show the boundary sharpen as training epochs tick up (animate a counter from epoch 0 to 100).
Scene 6: Convergence Test

Scene 7: MMD (Maximum Mean Discrepancy)
Show two overlapping point clouds in 2D — one red (David embeddings), one blue (Thomas embeddings). They overlap heavily. Label: "Are these distributions actually different?" Animate the MMD computation: sample points from each cloud, draw arrows between them representing pairwise kernel distances, then collapse all distances into a single scalar. Show a number line from 0 to 1 — our value lands somewhere small but statistically significant. Flash a p-value. "Small but real. The distributions are different."
Scene 8: Silhouette Score
Start with the same two point clouds. Pick a single point and highlight it. Draw two measurements: (a) average distance to its own cluster, labeled "cohesion," and (b) average distance to the nearest other cluster, labeled "separation." The silhouette score is (b − a) / max(a, b). Animate a bar from −1 (wrong cluster) to +1 (perfectly separated). Our score lands close to 0 but slightly positive — "weak but real clustering."

Scene 12: Softmax Zero-Sum Budget
Show a bar chart of predicted probabilities across 10 names, all summing to 100%. Animate what happens when the model gets more confident about one name — that bar grows, all others shrink. Then show a "hard" name like Robert: the model can't predict it reliably, so its probability mass gets redistributed to confident names. Robert's bar shrinks toward zero across training epochs. "The model doesn't just fail to learn Robert — it actively learns to not predict Robert."
